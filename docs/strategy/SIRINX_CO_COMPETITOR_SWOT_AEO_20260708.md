## Scope

This note reviews current public-positioning patterns for SIRINX.co against representative Thailand and Southeast Asia solar competitors. The focus is the public trust / SEO / AEO / lead-capture position for commercial solar carport, rooftop solar, BESS, EV charger, and AI energy management.

## Market Pattern

Competitor messaging is converging on four high-intent buyer themes:

1. No upfront investment / PPA / B.O.O.T. financing.
2. Enterprise proof through named projects, MWp, MWh, portfolio scale, and client categories.
3. Integrated energy transition: rooftop solar + BESS + EV charger + energy efficiency + monitoring.
4. Operational assurance: design, licensing, installation, O&M, monitoring, performance accountability.

SIRINX should avoid copying generic "solar installer" positioning and instead own a sharper niche:

> Engineering-led solar carport and AI energy systems for facilities that need roof, parking, EV load, BESS, and operating-cost control handled as one governed design.

## SWOT

### Strengths

- SIRINX already has a distinctive combined domain: Solar Carport / Rooftop Solar / BESS / EV Charger / AI Energy Management.
- LINE OA and website bot can become a faster lead-capture path than large corporate competitors with slower enterprise forms.
- Public website already has Thai-first commercial intent and province/local SEO surfaces.
- The existing calculator/assessment path can become a practical AEO answer engine if backed by clear assumptions and safe disclaimers.

### Weaknesses

- Current public proof is likely weaker than competitors that publish MWp, MWh, named enterprise projects, financing model, and portfolio totals.
- PPA/B.O.O.T./zero-capex positioning is not yet as crisp as GreenYellow, Constant Energy, and Cleantech Solar.
- Trust signals need clearer segmentation: factory, hotel, retail, warehouse, condominium, parking lot, and high-load residence.
- Technical claims must stay careful; do not publish guaranteed savings or certifications unless verified.

### Opportunities

- Create "send bill / send site photo / get engineering pre-check" as the fastest LINE-led conversion path.
- Build AEO pages that answer buyer questions directly:
  - Solar carport vs rooftop solar for factories.
  - When BESS is worth it in Thailand.
  - EV charger load planning for commercial parking.
  - PPA vs CAPEX vs lease model comparison.
  - What data engineers need before a quotation.
- Use project-proof templates that show facts without overclaiming:
  - site type
  - problem
  - system type
  - capacity if verified
  - design constraints
  - commissioning status
  - before/after operating insight if approved
- Turn `/assessment` and future `/roi-calculator` into a lead magnet with transparent assumptions, not a hard guarantee.

### Threats

- International PPA players can outrank on "no investment" and enterprise credibility.
- Public companies can outrank on trust due to listed-company status, portfolio scale, and institutional references.
- Generic solar installers can compete on price-first residential search, weakening lead quality.
- If SIRINX publishes unverified savings or ROI claims, it risks trust loss and compliance problems.

## Do Not Publish Without Evidence

- Guaranteed ROI.
- Guaranteed savings percentage.
- Fake reviews.
- Fake ratings.
- Unverified certification or partner logos.
- Named project figures without owner approval.
- Customer data, bills, locations, or photos without usage rights.
